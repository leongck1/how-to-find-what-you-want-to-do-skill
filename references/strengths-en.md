# Strengths (擅长的事) — Complete List

26 talent types, each shown as both a strength and a weakness — the same trait, two angles.
Use this to help users recognize their natural talents and reframe how they see their habits.

| # | Talent | When it's a Strength | When it's a Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Innovating (finding better ways) | Flexibly changes methods to work more effectively | Gets bored when nothing changes |
| 2 | Commander (orchestrating big events) | Can organize and run large-scale events | Hates routine tasks |
| 3 | Team Builder | Skilled at placing people to maximize team productivity | Can make those around feel confused |
| 4 | Improving | Supplements missing skills and knowledge | Gets exhausted trying to change people who can't be changed |
| 5 | Problem Solving | Identifies root causes and solves them | Lost when there's no problem to solve |
| 6 | Problem Finding | Confronts issues directly | Can come across as overly negative |
| 7 | Creating Order | Builds structure to improve efficiency | Struggles when unexpected things happen |
| 8 | Steady Execution | Consistently does what needs to be done | Has difficulty adapting to change |
| 9 | Planning | Follows through on plans carefully | Becomes anxious when plans fall apart |
| 10 | Fairness | Treats everyone equally | Cannot tolerate favoritism |
| 11 | Rule-Following | Acts correctly according to rules | Falls into confusion without rules |
| 12 | Rule-Making | Creates rules everyone can accept | Allows no exceptions |
| 13 | Careful Deliberation | Makes risk-free decisions after thorough discussion | Gets lost when there's no clear standard to judge by |
| 14 | Precision | Makes very few mistakes | Works slowly |
| 15 | Drawing Others Out | Encourages others to open up and share | Takes a long time to express their own thoughts |
| 16 | Strong Ethics | Acts for the good of the world and humanity | (No notable downside listed) |
| 17 | Consistency | Steers organizations back on track when they drift | Can become stubborn |
| 18 | Dedication | Trusted because they are not driven by money | Tends to sacrifice themselves |
| 19 | Responsibility | Trusted by those around them | Cannot say no to requests |
| 20 | Task Focus | Completes assigned tasks very well | Loses interest in work they don't personally endorse; doesn't know what to do without a clear assignment |
| 21 | Keeping Commitments | Strictly honors agreements with others | Demands others do the same |
| 22 | High Output | Keeps completing work continuously | Often anxious because they can't look back on past work |
| 23 | Efficiency | Doesn't waste time | Can become a workaholic |
| 24 | Energizing | Brings the team to life | Can exhaust others by expecting everyone to match their energy |
| 25 | Priority Setting | Makes clear what work comes first | Sees anything unrelated to the goal (socializing, leisure) as a waste of time |
| 26 | Goal Achievement | Moves straight toward the goal | Has no motivation without a goal |

> Note: Entries 27–30 in the original book are left blank for the user to fill in their own discovered talents.
