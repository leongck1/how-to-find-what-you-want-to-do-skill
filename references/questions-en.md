# Question Banks (问题清单)

The full self-awareness question banks — 30 questions each for Values, Strengths, and Passion.
Use these when guiding someone through discovery. Pull the relevant questions; you don't need to ask all 30.

When a question has a follow-up, it's shown as a sub-point — the prompt after the answer that digs toward the underlying value, strength, or passion.

---

## Values (重要的事)

> **Questions marked with `***` are specifically for finding your WORK PURPOSE** (the impact you want to have on others), not just your personal values. Prioritize these once you've drafted your core values.

1. Who, when you met them, struck you deeply? — Which of their traits struck you? Which of your values do those traits connect to?
2. Who has had the biggest influence on who you are now? — Which of their actions or words influenced you?
3. About your father's way of living — what do you like, and what do you dislike? Does your value set carry an echo of his, or do you treat his values as a counter-example?
4. About your mother's way of living — what do you like, and what do you dislike? (Same follow-up as above.)
5. After you die, how would you want the people around you to remember you?
6. Of all the books you've ever read, which is your favorite? — Which part of it, and what value does that reveal?
7. What kinds of things move you emotionally? — What has moved you most?
8. Imagine you're already 80. Fill in the blanks: "Because I was afraid of ___, I spent too much time on it. I spent almost no time on ___. If I could go back, I would spend more time on ___."
9. At work or in life, who do you respect the *least*? — Which traits can't you respect? The opposite of those traits points to your values.
10. From birth through primary school, what made you happiest?
11. Have you made any pivotal decisions in your life? — What was the judgment standard you valued most at the time?
12. What experience are you most proud of? (People feel pride when acting in line with their values.)
13. Who is your best friend, or a friend you were once close to? What do you like about them?
14. What's the hardest you've ever worked at something? — Which value was the source of that drive?
15. What brands do you love?
16. List the things you find interesting.
17. `***` In your life so far, what is the most unforgivable thing to you?
18. When do you feel happiest?
19. Five years from now, if anything you write down right now would come true, what would you write?
20. Have you made any important decisions so far? — What factors did you weigh when making them?
21. At work or in life, what makes you feel proud?
22. `***` Looking back over your life, what kind of impact do you want to bring to the people around you?
23. Is the way you currently spend your time meaningful to you? — If something's missing, what is it?
24. At work or in life, who do you respect the most? — Which of their traits do you respect?
25. `***` By sharing what, would you help the people around you?
26. `***` Who is the best leader you've known? — What did they do that makes you say so?
27. Who is the worst leader you've known? — What did they do that makes you say so?
28. What exists now that you believe shouldn't exist in your future life? (e.g. flattery, obligatory socializing, overeating, forcing yourself.)
29. `***` What makes you want to curse out loud in frustration? (The urge to swear comes from having seen an ideal state — the gap with reality frustrates you.)
30. `***` List 10 things that make you sigh "if only it were ___." About yourself, others, organizations, society — any angle.

## Strengths (擅长的事)

1. What were you naturally good at — or once good at — since childhood? (Recall primary school; write down concrete experiences.)
2. What do you do well without paying it much attention?
3. Recall a period when you were absorbed in something. — What kind of environment were you in?
4. When have people thanked you? (Describe specifically.) — What does that reveal you're good at?
5. Ask a friend: "What are my weaknesses?" — The answers may sting. Flip them: what does each weakness imply you're good at?
6. What's the biggest setback or deepest regret you've had? (Setback and regret come from spending effort without result.) — What strength does it reveal?
7. What do you like about yourself? (What you like about yourself is often tied to a strength.)
8. What do you find tricky or awkward? — Flipped over, what does it say you're good at?
9. Where do you feel you fall short right now? — What specifically made you feel that way? Flipped over, what's the hidden strength?
10. What can you do innately, easily, without ever tiring? (What you can do from birth is your strength.)
11. What do you catch yourself thinking "how can people *not* do this?" about? (You think this because it's an effortless strength you take for granted.)
12. Write down praise others have given you. — In what situations were you praised? What strength does it reveal?
13. How do others see your personality? — What do they think suits you? What strength does that reveal?
14. What has troubled or frustrated you for a long time? — What experience caused it? Flipped over, what hidden strength lies inside?
15. What kind of work pulls you into a state of flow? (People get absorbed doing what they're good at.)
16. What makes you feel excited? (People feel excited doing what they're good at.)
17. What do you do on your days off? (What you do naturally is your strength.)
18. What did parents and teachers keep reminding or scolding you about? (What others flag is often where you stand out.) — What strength does it reveal?
19. What work would you absolutely never want to do? — Does it reveal what you find hard, or what you're good at?
20. What can you do for a long time without tiring? (Though even this gets uncomfortable if pushed forever.)
21. When do you feel you're "being yourself"? (Doing strengths feels like being yourself.)
22. What did a recent happy, fulfilling day look like? (Doing strengths feels fulfilling.)
23. When with others, what have you been told you are? (This is the energy you unconsciously give people — the influence you radiate is your strength.)
24. What have you been praised for without putting in much effort?
25. What do you do almost subconsciously?
26. Reflect on your motivations for doing things. — What in your life do you do unconsciously? What strength does it reveal?
27. What do you do faster or better than others?
28. Doing what type of work do you feel most efficient?
29. Doing what makes you feel at ease and good?
30. What kind of task, work, or activity could you do endlessly without it wearing on you? Keep the bar low — list 10. (e.g. "I rarely read, but I can get through books on ___.")

## Passion (喜欢的事)

1. Setting aside conditions like work and money, what do you love?
2. Is there a topic that excites you, or something that fires you up?
3. Doing what makes you feel happy? — What does it reveal you love?
4. If money were no concern and anything could become a job, what work would you want to do? (For now, delete "can I / can't I" from your mind before answering.)
5. If any job were guaranteed to earn you others' respect, what would you do?
6. Of everything you've studied, what did you find interesting? — What does it reveal you love?
7. What would you choose to learn? — What does it reveal you love?
8. As a child, what job did you dream of doing when you grew up? — What about it attracted you? What does it reveal you love?
9. What games were you absorbed in as a child? (Things you did purely your own way, uninfluenced by others, are things you simply love.)
10. Is there something new you want to do now? — What does it reveal you love?
11. Of all the books you've read, which is your favorite? — What does it reveal you love?
12. What struggles have you already overcome? Or what problems do you want to solve going forward? — What does it reveal you love?
13. In today's society, what do you see as a problem? — What does it reveal you love?
14. Where have you spent more time than other people? — What does it reveal you love?
15. What do you like talking about with family and friends? — What does it reveal you love?
16. If you had a week off, what would you want to do? (Describe specifically.) — What does it reveal you love?
17. What seems expensive to most people but cheap to you? — What does it reveal you love?
18. What do you want to do but haven't yet? — What does it reveal you love?
19. What are your hobbies? (Write them for three stages — primary school, secondary/high school, now. Hobbies are things you'd do even without spending money. List everything, whether or not you're good at it.) — What does it reveal you love?
20. What do you search for often? (Write it for the same three life stages.) — What does it reveal you love?
21. What were your favorite subjects in school? — What does it reveal you love?
22. What TV shows do you, or did you, love? — What does it reveal you love?
23. What thing, genre, or person gave you the feeling "this saved me!"?
24. Ask family or friends: "What fields do I seem interested in?"
25. What makes you ask "why?" and "how?"
26. Looking back, what theme have you held a continuous interest in?
27. Recall your work purpose. — What field looks like it could realize your values? Are you interested in that field?
28. What kind of task, work, or activity can you absolutely *not* enjoy? — What does it reveal you *dislike*?
29. Think from a third party's point of view. — What does this person (you) usually seem interested in?
30. What fields of people do you follow online? (In the areas where you keep consuming information, there's something you're deeply interested in.)
