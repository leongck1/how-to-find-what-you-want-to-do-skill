## The Book

### *How to Find What You Want to Do* (《如何找到想做的事》)
**Jinpei Yagi (八木仁平)** | Translated by 徐艺菊

The book is built around a single, deceptively simple promise: what you want to do is not something you stumble upon by destiny — it can be found systematically, through a logical method of self-understanding. The author argues that people stay lost not because they lack passion, but because they've never learned to categorize what they already know about themselves.

The method rests on three pillars — **Values (the "why"), Strengths (the "how"), and Passion (the "what")** — and their intersection is what the author calls your "true calling." Through three rules, five-step exercises, and extensive question banks, the book walks readers from confusion to a concrete sense of direction. It's aimed at anyone still "searching for themselves": new graduates choosing a path, mid-career professionals considering a change, and people returning to work.

The original Japanese edition was featured on Nakata Atsuhiko's *YouTube University*, surpassed 300,000 copies in Japan, and has spread to China, Korea, and Hong Kong for a worldwide total of over 600,000 copies.

## The Author

### Jinpei Yagi (八木仁平)
Born in 1993 in Kochi Prefecture, Japan, Jinpei Yagi graduated from the Faculty of Commerce at Waseda University. After university he made his living as a freelance blogger — earning over ¥1 million a month — but lost any sense of purpose in his work beyond money and fell into depression. To find what he genuinely wanted to do, he read more than 100 books and spent some ¥3 million attending seminars, eventually building his own systematic method out of what he learned.

He went on to found **Jikorikai Inc. (株式会社ジコリカイ)**, a Tokyo-based self-understanding education company established in 2017 (also referred to as Meee Inc.). He is a Gallup-certified Strengths Coach. His blog and the method behind it drew tens of millions of views, and he developed a multi-step "Self-Understanding Program" that helps roughly 200 students and working professionals a year find what they truly want to do. *How to Find What You Want to Do* was the first book to put that program into print.

### What's in SKILL.md

The skill covers all eight chapters of the book, organized around the Self-Awareness Method:

| Chapter | Topic | Key Tool(s) / Concept(s) |
|---------|-------|--------------------------|
| 1 | The 5 misconceptions that block you | Misconception → Truth table |
| 2 | Why we stay lost | Jam Study (too many options); "want to" vs "should" |
| 3 | The Self-Awareness Method | The core formula (Values × Strengths × Passion); the 3 Rules |
| 4 | Values — the compass | Life Purpose vs Work Purpose; 5-step values process; the overflow principle |
| 5 | Strengths — apply them to work | Strengths vs Skills; weakness→strength reframe; speed-reading study; Halo Effect; 5 questions |
| 6 | Passion — saying goodbye to forced effort | Interest- vs utility-based passion; marathon vs sprint; 5 questions |
| 7 | Your true calling | Passion × Strengths → filter by Work Purpose; bending the method to your strengths |
| 8 | The magic of self-awareness | Color Bath Effect; failure → experience; success = living as yourself |

Plus: a **"How to Use This Skill"** coaching guide up top (how to run it as a guided conversation rather than a lecture), a **Career Decisions Framework**, and pointers into the four reference files.
